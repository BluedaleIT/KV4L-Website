    Changkat, Bukit Bintang

    Hosting heaps of themed nights, talented DJs, and live band shows, it's safe to say that Changkat Bukit Bintang's nightlife is anything but boring. Among the many watering holes available here, there are a few establishments that stand out because of their unique concepts. Stand-up comedy, international film screenings and open mic nights are just some of the activities you can find in this area.

    Bangsar

    Sitting in the middle of Klang Valley, Bangsar and Mid Valley play hosts to a number of trendy resto-bars, pubs, and cocktail lounges. Most of these nightlife hotspots are concentrated around Jalan Telawi in Bangsar Baru and are within walking distances from each other. A 10- minute drive from KL city centre, the area is always bustling with late-night revellers every Friday until Sunday. Always bustling regardless of the time of the day, nightfall turns the atmosphere even more electrifying, with bars promising to be hopping long into the small hours of the morning. Attracting a diverse mix of locals and expatriates, Bangsar's position only four kilometres from KL Sentral Station makes it convenient for visitors.

    Solaris Dutamas

    This place is another great host to pubs and resto-bars in Klang Valley. A 10-minute drive from Jalan Duta toll. Solaris Dutamas also some of the high-end brands on their premises. So, if you can shop till you drop (well, maybe not - it totally depends on you). After that, you head over to one of the pubs or resto-bars to have some drinks. Some bars on the ground floor gives you the boulevard feels. So if you’re around Kuala Lumpur, and need a quick drink head over to Solaris Dutamas. You will be spoilt for choices.

    Petaling Street

    Petaling Street is a league of its own. It's not about rows of clubs, pubs and bars providing music-and-alcohol-fuelled entertainment, but the lively after-dark market that dominates the nightlife scene here. The entire area is turned into a lively and vibrant night market when the sun sets. You can also find a variety of nightlife hotspots here, from inns complete with outdoor rooftop bars to chatelaines and cocktail bars are hidden along narrow alleyways.

    Petaling Jaya

    If you don’t want to head over to the city centre, but still want to spend the night out head over to Petaling Jaya. One of the densest cities within the Klang Valley it is home to some of the most exciting nightspots in Malaysia. Most of these nightlife hotspots are concentrated around PJ's suburban locales such as Taman Tun Dr Ismail (TTDI) and Bandar Utama. Petaling Jaya is host to relaxed suburban pubs, live music venues, bustling nightclubs on the rooftop, and trendy gastro lounges offering a wide variety of drinks. Nightlife attracts a steady clientele of locals and ex-pats due to its attractive happy hour promos, themed ladies nights and welcoming ambience.

    The Chinatown

    As one of the friendly neighbourhood bars in Damansara Jaya, The Chinatown has become a trusted spot to spend the night out in town. In terms of light recreational activities, there is a beer pong, pool table and even live bands usually organised. While Chinese and Western food is served here, there is also an optional Omakase style meal available.

    Location:
    Ground Floor, No.12 & 14, Jalan SS 22/25, Damansara Jaya, 47400 Petaling Jaya, Selangor

    12.00 pm - 10.00 pm (Monday - Sunday)

Regardless, kick back your feet and enjoy some booze and signature dishes here.

    Setia Alam Night Market

    If visitors are looking to explore the local culture up close and personal, look no further than the Setia Alam Night Market. Stretched across 2.4 kilometres, this may be considered as the longest night market in the country. With a wide variety of food and snacks available here, this is a must-visit for the ultimate foodie.

    Location:
    Jalan Setia Prima A U13/A, Setia Alam, 40170 Shah Alam, Selangor
    Operating Hours:
    4.30 pm - 10.00 pm (Saturday)

    Pasar Malam SS2

    If visitors are looking to get a taste of the local culture up close and personal, it is best to head over to Pasar Malam SS2. With a wide variety of food and snacks available here, this is a must-visit for the ultimate foodie. On top of that, gadgets and clothing are also sold here.

    Location:

    Kota Damansara Friday Night Market

    If visitors are looking to explore the local culture up close and personal, look no further than the Kota Damansara Friday Night Market. Although this is a relatively small market place, there is a wide variety of food and snacks available here – both halal and non-halal.

    Location:
    Jalan TSB 9, Taman Industri Sungai Buloh, 47000 Sungai Buloh, Selangor
    Operating Hours:

    Pasar Malam Taman Megah

    If visitors are looking to get a taste of the local culture up close and personal, it is recommended to head over to Pasar Malam Taman Megah.
