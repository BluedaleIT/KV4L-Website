    FRIM

    The Forest Research Institute of Malaysia is a world-renowned centre for tropical forestry research. It has over 100 years of experience in forestry and forest products research.

    Read More

    Jogging, hiking and cycling are the most prominent activities here, and bicycles are available for hire at affordable rates. For the protection of its visitors, it should be noted that mountain biking is restricted to designated trails within Bukit Bujang, Simpang K, Perah Campsite, Tongkat Ali or Bukit Steroid, and Puncak Pinus.

    Read Less

    Forest Research Institute Malaysia (FRIM), 68100 Kuala Lumpur, Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur
    Operating Hours:
    8.00 am - 5.00 pm (Monday - Friday)
    Closed on Saturday & Sunday

    Templer's Park

    The Kanching Waterfalls, also known as Templer's Park Falls, are situated 22 km north of Kuala Lumpur. It is the ideal location for bird watching, jungle trekking and swimming. The million-year-old limestone hill of Bukit Takun is also nearby.

    Read More

    Templer's Park is also a convenient escape from the rigours of urban life. Templer's Park provides calm, quiet and relaxation with fresh air and beautiful beauty. The waterfall itself, which descends in two cascades, is good enough, even though the nearby Kanching falls are higher and more spectacular.

    Read Less


    Location:
    1, 48000 Rawang, Selangor
    Operating Hours:
    7.00 am- 8.00 pm (Daily)
    Phone:
    +603-5513 2000

    Lepoh Waterfalls

    Air Terjun Sungai Lepoh is an accessible hiking, waterfall and picnic site located in Selangor State Park. Lepoh Waterfalls are especially famous for its natural 'slide' which is a wide, flat and incredibly smooth rock formation.

    Read More

    The waterfall is nestled in a lush green environment in the Hulu Langat district of Selangor, a region renowned for its nature and outdoor activities. There is an entrance fee of RM1.00 that is collected when you register before starting your hike. Separate parking charges shall apply

    Read Less


    Location:
    43100 Hulu Langat, Selangor
    Operating Hours:
    7.00 am - 7.00 pm (Daily)

    Taman Eko Rimba, Kuala Lumpur

    Kuala Lumpur Forest Eco Park is a free eco-tourism destination in Malaysia. Visitors can camp, hike, and even conduct some research there. Instagram worthy canopy walk overlooks the forest and surrounding skyscrapers.

    Read More

    This free and Instagrammable park is only 3 kilometres away from the heart of Kuala Lumpur. You can find private parking for around RM 10.00 in Jalan Raja Chulan which is just a stone’s throw from the park.

    Read Less

    50250 Kuala Lumpur, Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur

    Chilling Waterfalls

    Often lauded as the most beautiful waterfall in Selangor, the Chiling Waterfalls and Santuari Ikan Sungai Chiling (Mahseer Fish Sanctuary) are located just over an hour's drive from Kuala Lumpur.

    Read More

    Do not miss the opportunity to say hello to this natural spot which houses three impressive vertical waterfalls. Located 21 kilometres from the quaint town of Kuala Kubu Bharu, you'll need to trek through the forest for about an hour to reach the waterfalls and cross the river multiple times. This journey is a fairly easy one, with the deepest crossing at waist-high, you don't have to worry about straining yourself through it. Once you've arrived, dive into the vast natural pool of waterfalls and feel rejuvenated by the cool, crisp waters. Whether or not you're an outdoor lover, the beauty of the waterfalls here leaves you breathless!

    Read Less


    Location:
    Sungai Chiling Fish Sanctuary, 44000 Kuala Kubu Bharu, Selangor
    Operating Hours:
    8.00 am – 6.00 pm (Saturday & Sunday)
    Closed during public holidays

    Sungai Tekala Waterfall

    Hidden away in the Hulu Langat district, find a piece of paradise called the Sungai Tekala Waterfall. Located within a tropical rainforest, this waterfall in Selangor can be reached from Ulu Langat or Semenyih.

    Read More

    The crystal clear waters of the shallow Sungai Tekala is suitable for swimming and simple changing facilities are available for the convenience of tourists. The upper levels of the river lead to a shimmering waterfall cascaded into a natural rock bowl. The Sungai Tekala Recreational Forest is home to this nature reserve and is the ideal place for a picnic, camping and trekking. Other facilities are open, such as resting huts that make fun shelters from the hot sun and benches for you to relax. If you're not a crowd fan, make sure to visit on off-peak days instead.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Sungai Tekala Waterfall, Selangor
    Website:
    www.waterfallsofmalaysia.com
    Operating Hours:
    7.00 am - 7.00 pm (Daily)

    Serendah Waterfall

    Tucked secretly 3 kilometres away from the indigenous village named Perkampungan Orang Asli Serendah, lies Serendah Waterfall. Also known as Templer Kai, you will be able to get a glimpse of the Orang Asli lifestyle as you drive to the waterfall.

    Read More

    Although the waterfall can seem small and shallow, the gushing water of the cascades is a soothing splash for those who sit by it. Also a perfect place for picnics and swimming in Selangor, you'll see a lot of families spread around the area with floating tubes and food baskets! Just at the junction of the waterfall, is where the popular Seven Well or Perigi Tujuh is located.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Kampung Orang Asli Serendah, 48200 Serendah, Selangor
    Phone:
    +6017-609 1504

    Sungai Tua Waterfalls

    Made up of swimming and wading pools, rapids, waterfalls and a near-vertical plunge, Sungai Tua Falls is perfect for all sorts of activities-picnics, swimming, barbecues, camping and more! Found within the Sungai Tua Recreational Forest.

    Read More

    Driving to these waterfalls is a scenic experience in itself, offering views of the rainforests and the majestic Batu Dam. Reserve, which in turn forms part of Selangor State Park, is a very popular spot among locals. You can also see the River Stone Eco and Nur Laman Bestari Eco Resorts nearby, where you can have a nice meal after enjoying the waterfalls. In the midst of lush jungles, flora, and massive bamboo growths, there is also a suspension bridge over the large stream that feeds the waterfall.

    Read Less


    Location:
    B23 Jalan Hulu Yam Bharu - Batu Caves, 68100 Batu Caves, Selangor

    7.00 am - 6.00 pm (Daily)
    Phone:
    +6010-518 7739

    Sungai Gabai Waterfall

    Allow the serene atmosphere and the lush greenery of the Sungai Gabai Waterfall sweep you into a calm state of tranquilly. Located in Selangor State Park, this waterfall in Selangor is a popular spot for a fast getaway from the city. As one of Kuala Lumpur's closest waterfalls, which can also be easily reached by car, this natural attraction is a convenient option when planning a refreshing getaway.

    Read More

    Walk along the wooden and concrete bridges over the streams to hit the top of Sungai Gabai's multi-cascade waterfalls. Then climb the cement stairs next to the waterfalls and soak up the marvellous view that awaits you at the top. Wade in the pool at the foot of the waterfall or go higher up past the end of the stairs for more pools to dive in. Usually, there are fewer crowds in the morning, so it's worth coming early!

    Read Less


    Location:
    Sungai Gabai Waterfall, 43100 Hulu Langat, Selangor
    Phone:
    +6014-351 7342

    Kanching Eco Forest Park

    At Kanching Eco Forest Park, witness seven planes of falls and pools at the massive waterfall located deep inside the forest reserve.

    Read More

    Although there is a clear cement footpath leading up to the first four, the more daring can try hiking up the challenging hill to reach the remaining pools. There are also gazebos, stone benches and a bridge built across a stream near the entrance that’s perfect for a picnic amid verdant greenery.

    Read Less
