    Orang Asli Crafts Museum

    The Orang Asli Craft Museum in Kuala Lumpur displays the arts and crafts of the native people of Malaysia. The museum describes how 18 separate tribes of Orang Asli are divided into three major groups.

    Read More

    The Orang Asli are recognised for their handwork, in particular the wood carving skills of the Mah Meri and Jah Hut tribes. There are small Sepili figures used in the traditional medicine system of the Jah Hut, blowpipes and ornate bamboo containers.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Jabatan Muzium Malaysia, Jalan Damansara, 50566 Kuala Lumpur
    Operating Hours:
    9.00 am - 6.00 pm (Daily)
    Phone:
    +603-2282 6255

    MinNature Malaysia

    MinNature Malaysia opened in November 2016 after eight years of planning, fundraising, designing and construction. It is the largest permanent indoor miniature and train display in Asia and the third-largest in the world. The eye for detail which has been given by MinNature 3D printers is quite remarkable.

    Read More

    Most of the figurines in this exhibition were designed from scratch in plastic. The figurines were made using their own 3D printers. The display space is split into different areas, the first of which is named Countryside Life.

    Dataran Merdeka

    Merdeka Square (Dataran Merdeka) is unquestionably Malaysia's best-known location. It's the core of KL's history. The Malayan flag was hoisted at this very spot on 31 August 1957.

    Read More

    That is why this spot is known as Dataran Merdeka which directly translates to Independence Square and has been where the Independence day parade took place until it was later moved to Putrajaya. Merdeka Square appears to be nothing more than a colossal field of beautifully manicured green lawns and the centrepiece of the tallest flagpole in the world (95 m) proudly displaying the Jalur Gemilang.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Dataran Merdeka, Jalan Raja, 57000 Kuala Lumpur
    Operating Hours:
    Open 24 hours

    Kwai Chai Hong

    If you're looking for a cool place to take photos to be uploaded on your social media platforms you have to pay a visit to Kwai Chai Hong. Not only will you find spots to take pictures but you will be also transported back to history.

    Read More

    Translating from Cantonese, Kwai Chai Hong means 'ghost street' or 'little demon alley,' the contested source of the name may be less nefarious and more mischievous in nature, referring to the children who used to make Lorong Panggung their playground. As you walk into the cosy little lane, you're transported back to the 1960s. Be enthralled by captivating murals full of secret stories and heavily hanging Chinese lanterns while walking on the red wooden bridge.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Lorong Panggung, City Centre, 50000 Kuala Lumpur, Wilayah Persekutuan Kuala Lumpur
    Operating Hours:
    9.00 am- 10.00 pm (Daily)
    Phone:
    +6012-316 6212

    Royal Museum

    The Old National Palace was transformed into a museum known as the Royal Museum in 2013. It was previously the residence of His Majesty Yang di-Pertuan Agong and His Majesty Raja Permaisuri Agong.

    Read More

    The Royal Museum was the mansion of the Chinese millionaire, Chan Wing, in 1928. During the Japanese occupation of Malaya, it was used as an officer\'s quarter by the Japanese. In 1950, the building was converted into a temporary palace for the Sultan of Selangor. The building was eventually bought by the Federal Government and turned into the National Palace.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Jalan Istana, Istana Negara, Kuala Lumpur, Federal Territory of Kuala Lumpur
    Operating Hours:
    9.00 am - 5.00 pm (Daily)
    Phone:
    +603-2272 1896

    Mah Meri Cultural Village

    The ancient Mah Meri tribe is one of the 18 native groups living in Peninsular Malaysia. Some of their handicrafts have been known to attract thousands of dollars. Pulau Carey has become a place for all to learn about their community, heritage and history.

    Read More

    Known as the Masked Men of Malaysia, they're known for their mask craftsmanship, and you'll see why they're decorated with impressive handicrafts throughout the cultural centre. There is a small gallery/museum and a traditional house in the village complex that is open to tourists. Although many natives have moved into modern life, working in nearby plantations and fields, a great deal of care has been made to make sure that their ancient practises and traditions are recorded and preserved.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Jalan Kampong Orang Asli Sungai Bumbun, Kampung Manikavasagam, 42960 Pulau Carey, Selangor
    Website:
    mmcv.org.my
    Operating Hours:
    9.00 am- 5.00 pm (Saturday - Sunday)
    Closed on Weekdays
    Phone:
    +6010-252 2800

    Hatter’s Castle

    Inspired by Scottish author A.J. Cronin’s novel of the same name, Hatter’s Castle is a four-storey cottage bungalow built during the early 1920s British colonisation period.

    Read More

    The design is believed to be influenced by the founder of Carey Island, Edward Valentine John Carey. It is currently maintained by Golden Hope Plantation as the farm owner, and used mainly as a staff and company guest house for Sime Darby Plantation.

    Tan Boon Chia Building

    The Tiffany blue hued Tan Boon Chia building is located in the once flourishing tin area known as Kuala Kubu Bharu, Hulu Selangor.

    Read More

    Constructed by tin mining tycoon Tan Boo Chia, this 51-room structure was built in 1918. It once housed the administrative headquarters of Malaya, which was then conquered by the Japanese during WWII. Visitors may currently only observe and photograph it from the outside.

    Read Less


    Location:
    Taman Rasa Jaya, 44200 Rasa, Hulu Selangor, Selangor
    Operating Hours:
    Open 24 hours

    Istana Bandar

    Constructed in 1899, Istana Bandar was commissioned by Sultan Ala'eddin Sulaiman Shah Ibni Al-Marhum Raja Muda Musa after the death of his nephew.

    Read More

    From his residential home, it became the Selangor government’s administrative center; later turned into a craft and religion center. The double-storey palace with 44 rooms and a forbidden garden has been injected with a blend of Asian and colonial influences.

    Wat Chetawan

    Also referred to as the Thai Buddhist Chetawan Temple, Wat Chetawan is a magnificent Thai Buddhist temple complex.

    Read More

    Perdana Botanical Garden

    Kuala Lumpur's Perdana Botanical Garden is the oldest public park in the city. The 225-acre site is home to a bird park, hibiscus and orchid garden and deer park. The main attraction is the central lake surrounded by lush greenery.

    Read More

    Kuala Lumpur's Perdana Botanical Garden has a history dating back over a decade. The gardens comprise local and foreign plants, with areas allocated to ferns, rare trees, medicinal herbs and aquatic plants. There are plenty of walking paths and benches for strolling around and relaxing in the shade.     Malaysia's largest indoor theme park is situated on the fifth floor of the Berjaya Times Square Mall. The 35,300 square metre park is divided into two sections: the Galaxy Station is built for adults and the Fantasy Garden for children.

    The 380,000 square metre park is split into two categories: the Galaxy Station is built for adults, while the Fantasy Garden is distinctly child-centric. There are four thrill rides, each built specifically for the Berjaya Times Square Theme Park World. The Galaxy Station sees the majority of visitors on a weekly basis but is off-limit for those below the age of 13.
